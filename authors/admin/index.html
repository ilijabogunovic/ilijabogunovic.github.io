Ilija Bogunovic

Department of Electronic and Electrical Engineering

University College London

I am an Assistant Professor (or UK Lecturer) at University College London (UCL) EEE. My research primarily focuses on algorithmic decision-making in the context of robust, reliable, and safe AI. I am interested in questions such as: How can we efficiently make decisions and discover policies, designs, and solutions that can withstand distributional shifts (sim-to-real gap), corruptions, adversaries, and model misspecifications? How can we exploit the potential of foundation models to facilitate robust and aligned decision-making? In the context of real-world machine learning, how can we realize reliability and robustness considerations within interactive data-driven algorithms (for reinforcement learning, Bayesian optimization/experiment design and multi-agent learning)? My work has been recognized through a Google Research Scholar Program Award and EPSRC New Investigator Award.

I am actively looking for motivated students at undergrad, MSc, and PhD levels, that are excited about Robust AI and algorithmic decision-making. Take a look at the publications section to get a sense of the type of research I am interested in. Feel free to reach out by sending a short email (with a summary of your interests + CV)!
